Research

Inverse Projection and Decision Maps
  • Projections:
    • A projection reduces high-dimensional data to lower dimensions, mapping data from \( \mathbb{R}^n \) to \( \mathbb{R}^q \), often using \( q = 2 \) for visualization purposes (e.g., 2D scatterplots).
    • Different projection techniques preserve data relationships with varying levels of computational speed and noise robustness.
  • Inverse Projections:
    • Inverse projections attempt to reverse the mapping of a projection, approximating the original high-dimensional data from its low-dimensional representation in \( \mathbb{R}^q \).
    • They are generally more complex due to the non-injective nature of projections and are used in tasks like data imputation and shape morphing.
  • Decision Maps:
    • Decision maps visually represent the decision boundaries of machine learning models by coloring pixels in a 2D space, showing how models categorize data points.
    • They are helpful in understanding model behavior and decision boundaries in explainable AI.
